# Course description

The Big Data and Analytics course is located in the intermediate cycle of the professional training area of ​​the degree program and belongs to the area of ​​technology and data science. It is a practical course that introduces the main concepts of Big Data, where students will create reports with information generated from the exploration and cleaning of large databases, contributing to the organization's decision-making.
The learning methodology will consist of practical classes in computer labs. The evaluation will consist of individual tests and group projects.

# General learning outcome

Prepares reports with information generated from the exploration and cleaning of large databases, which contribute to the organization's decision-making.

# Course Unit 1

## Conceptual Resources Involved

* Definition and components of information systems.
* Importance of information systems in organizations.
* Actors and roles in information systems.
* Types of management information systems: DSS, BMI, CMR, EIS, among others.

## Procedural Resources Involved

* Classification of information system components according to their function in data processing and storage.
* Establishing the importance of information systems in organizations, considering their impact on strategic decision-making.
* Classification of actors and roles in information systems according to their level of responsibility and access to information.
* Explanation of the types of management information systems based on their application at different organizational levels.
* Demonstration of integrity and ethical conduct in the development of academic activities, assessments, and professional internships.
